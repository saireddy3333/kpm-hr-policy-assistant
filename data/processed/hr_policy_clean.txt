Kentucky Precision Manufacturing (KPM) – HR Policy

1. Introduction & Company Overview

1.1 Purpose of This Manual
This Human Resources (HR) Policy Manual has been developed to provide employees of Kentucky Precision Manufacturing (KPM) with a clear understanding of the company’s expectations, policies, and procedures. It serves as a comprehensive reference guide covering employment practices, workplace conduct, compensation, benefits, safety, and compliance requirements.
The manual is designed to support a consistent and fair work environment while helping employees understand their rights and responsibilities. It also ensures that KPM maintains compliance with federal, state, and local regulations, including Kentucky labor laws and industry-specific safety standards.
While this manual outlines important guidelines, it is not an employment contract. KPM reserves the right to modify, revoke, or add policies at any time based on operational needs, regulatory changes, or business priorities. Employees will be notified of significant updates as they occur.

1.2 Company Overview
Kentucky Precision Manufacturing (KPM) is a mid-sized automotive components supplier located in Kentucky, serving major automotive manufacturers including Ford and Toyota. With approximately 200 employees across production, logistics, maintenance, engineering, HR, accounting, and administrative functions, KPM plays a critical role in the regional automotive supply chain.
Our operations require a high degree of precision, consistency, and reliability. Automotive manufacturing demands tight delivery schedules, rigorous quality standards, and strict adherence to safety protocols. Every employee contributes to maintaining KPM’s reputation for excellence, whether working on the production line, supporting operations, or managing administrative functions.
KPM is committed to fostering a workplace culture built on respect, teamwork, accountability, and continuous improvement. We believe that a safe, inclusive, and supportive environment enables employees to perform at their best and helps the company meet the demanding expectations of the automotive industry.

1.3 Policy Scope
Unless otherwise stated, all policies in this manual apply to:
Full-time employees
Part-time employees
Temporary employees
Seasonal employees
Supervisors and managers
Staff employees in administrative or support roles
Contractors, vendors, and visitors must comply with all applicable safety, security, and conduct rules while on company premises. Certain policies—such as those related to safety, confidentiality, and technology use—apply to anyone accessing KPM facilities, systems, or equipment.

1.4 Policy Changes and Interpretation
KPM reserves the right to revise, update, or discontinue policies at any time. Changes may be driven by:
Regulatory updates
Industry standards
Operational needs
Safety requirements
Organizational restructuring
When policies are updated, employees will be notified through email, posted notices, or supervisor communication. The most current version of the HR Policy Manual will always supersede previous versions.
Supervisors and HR representatives are available to clarify any policy. When a policy refers to another section of the manual—for example, safety procedures in Section 7 or leave policies in Section 5—employees should review the referenced section for additional detail.

1.5 Employee Responsibilities
Employees are expected to:
Read and understand the policies in this manual
Seek clarification when needed
Follow all workplace rules and procedures
Report safety concerns or policy violations promptly
Contribute to a respectful and productive work environment
Failure to follow company policies may result in disciplinary action as outlined in Section 10.

1.6 Company Responsibilities
KPM is committed to:
Providing a safe and healthy workplace
Ensuring fair and consistent application of policies
Complying with all applicable laws and regulations
Supporting employee development and performance
Maintaining open communication channels
The HR department is responsible for maintaining this manual, ensuring compliance, and supporting employees and supervisors in understanding and applying the policies.

1.7 Acknowledgment of Receipt
All employees must sign an acknowledgment confirming they have received, read, and understood the HR Policy Manual. This acknowledgment does not constitute a contract but confirms awareness of the company’s expectations and guidelines.

2. Employment Policies

2.1 Employment Classifications
To ensure clarity in job expectations, compensation, and eligibility for benefits, Kentucky Precision Manufacturing (KPM) classifies employees into several categories. These classifications help determine work schedules, overtime eligibility, and access to company programs. Employees will be informed of their classification during onboarding, and any changes will be communicated by HR.
Full-Time Employees
Full-time employees are regularly scheduled to work 30 hours or more per week. They are eligible for the full range of company benefits, including health insurance, retirement plans, paid time off, and other programs described in Section 5.
Part-Time Employees
Part-time employees are scheduled to work fewer than 30 hours per week. They may be eligible for certain benefits depending on hours worked and plan requirements. Part-time employees are expected to follow all workplace rules and safety procedures.
Temporary Employees
Temporary employees are hired for a defined period, project, or seasonal need. They may be employed directly by KPM or through a staffing agency. Temporary employees are not eligible for most company benefits but must comply with all safety, conduct, and confidentiality policies.
Seasonal Employees
Seasonal employees support production during peak automotive demand cycles. Their employment duration varies based on customer orders and manufacturing schedules. Seasonal employees must follow all safety and attendance requirements due to the fast-paced nature of automotive production.
Exempt and Non-Exempt Status
Under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA):
Exempt employees are paid a salary and are not eligible for overtime.
Non-exempt employees are paid hourly and receive overtime pay for hours worked over 40 in a workweek.
Employees will be informed of their status during onboarding. Questions about classification should be directed to HR.
Probationary Period
All new hires complete a 90-day probationary period. This period allows employees to become familiar with KPM’s expectations and gives supervisors time to evaluate performance, attendance, and overall fit. Successful completion of probation does not guarantee continued employment, but it does confirm that the employee has met initial performance expectations.

2.2 Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO)
KPM is committed to maintaining a workplace free from discrimination and harassment. The company provides equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants regardless of:
Race
Color
Religion
Sex (including pregnancy, childbirth, and related conditions)
National origin
Age
Disability
Genetic information
Veteran status
Any other protected characteristic under federal or Kentucky law
Employment decisions—including hiring, promotion, compensation, and training—are based on qualifications, performance, and business needs.
Reasonable Accommodations
KPM will provide reasonable accommodations to qualified individuals with disabilities, as required by the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). Employees needing accommodations should contact HR. Supervisors must not deny or modify accommodations without HR involvement.
Anti-Retaliation
Retaliation against employees who report discrimination, participate in investigations, or request accommodations is strictly prohibited. Concerns should be reported to HR or a supervisor immediately. See Section 6.2 for additional details on harassment and reporting procedures.

2.3 Recruitment & Hiring
KPM strives to attract and hire individuals who demonstrate the skills, experience, and values needed to support our high-precision automotive manufacturing environment.
Job Postings and Applications
Open positions are posted internally and externally. Employees are encouraged to apply for roles that match their qualifications. Applications must be complete and accurate; falsification may result in disqualification or termination.
Interview and Selection Process
Candidates are evaluated based on:
Relevant experience
Technical skills
Safety awareness
Reliability and attendance history
Cultural fit and teamwork
Supervisors and HR representatives participate in the selection process to ensure fairness and consistency.
Background Checks
KPM may conduct background checks, including employment verification, criminal history, and drug screening, depending on the role. Offers of employment may be contingent upon successful completion of these checks.
Eligibility to Work
All employees must complete the Form I-9 and provide documentation verifying their identity and eligibility to work in the United States.
Nepotism Policy
To avoid conflicts of interest, relatives may not be hired into positions where one relative would directly supervise another or influence employment decisions. HR must approve any exceptions.

2.4 Employment Records
KPM maintains personnel files for each employee. These files contain employment applications, performance evaluations, training records, disciplinary actions, and other relevant documents.
Access to Personnel Files
Employees may request to review their personnel file by contacting HR. Reviews must occur in the presence of an HR representative. Employees may request copies of documents they have signed.
Confidentiality of Records
Personnel files are confidential and accessible only to authorized HR staff and supervisors with a legitimate business need. Employee information will not be shared externally unless required by law or with the employee’s written consent.
Updating Personal Information
Employees must promptly notify HR of changes to:
Address
Phone number
Emergency contacts
Tax withholding status
Dependent information
Accurate records ensure proper communication and benefits administration.

2.5 Employment-At-Will Statement
Employment with KPM is at-will, meaning either the employee or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without notice, and with or without cause, unless otherwise prohibited by law. Nothing in this manual creates a contract or alters the at-will nature of employment.

3. Work Hours, Attendance & Scheduling

3.1 Standard Work Hours
Kentucky Precision Manufacturing (KPM) operates in a fast-paced automotive supply environment where production schedules are closely tied to customer demand from Ford, Toyota, and other major manufacturers. To meet these expectations, KPM maintains structured work hours designed to support consistent output, predictable staffing, and safe operations.
The standard workweek for full-time employees consists of 40 hours, typically arranged across five 8-hour shifts. Production employees may be assigned to one of several shifts depending on operational needs:
Day Shift
Evening Shift
Night Shift
Weekend or rotating shifts during peak demand periods
Shift assignments are determined by department supervisors and may change based on production requirements, customer orders, or staffing levels. Employees will receive advance notice of shift changes whenever possible.
Meal and Rest Breaks
KPM provides meal and rest breaks in accordance with federal and Kentucky labor guidelines. Break schedules are designed to ensure safety, maintain production flow, and support employee well-being.
Meal Break: Typically 30 minutes, unpaid
Rest Breaks: Two paid 10–15 minute breaks per shift (varies by department)
Employees must return from breaks on time to avoid disrupting production schedules. Breaks may not be combined, extended, or used to leave early unless approved by a supervisor.
Shift Start Expectations
Employees are expected to be at their workstation, in proper attire, and ready to begin work at the start of their scheduled shift. For production employees, this includes wearing required personal protective equipment (PPE) as outlined in Section 6.5.

3.2 Attendance & Punctuality
Consistent attendance is essential in automotive manufacturing, where production lines depend on every team member being present and prepared. Absences or tardiness can disrupt workflow, delay shipments, and impact customer satisfaction.
Reporting Absences
Employees must notify their supervisor before the start of their shift if they will be absent or late. Notification should occur as early as possible to allow supervisors to adjust staffing. Failure to report an absence may be considered a no-call/no-show, which is a serious violation of company policy.
Tardiness
Employees are considered tardy if they arrive after their scheduled start time. Repeated tardiness may result in corrective action as outlined in Section 10.
Unscheduled Absences
Unscheduled absences should be limited to emergencies or unavoidable circumstances. Excessive unscheduled absences may impact performance evaluations and could result in disciplinary action.
Attendance Records
Supervisors maintain attendance records for all employees. Employees may request a review of their attendance history through HR.

3.3 Overtime
Due to the nature of automotive manufacturing, overtime may be required to meet customer deadlines, respond to increased demand, or recover from production disruptions. KPM strives to balance operational needs with employee well-being.
Eligibility
Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5 times their regular hourly rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek.
Exempt employees are not eligible for overtime compensation.
Overtime Approval
All overtime must be pre-approved by a supervisor. Unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action, even if the hours were worked.
Mandatory Overtime
In certain situations—such as urgent customer orders, equipment downtime recovery, or supply chain delays—mandatory overtime may be required. Supervisors will provide as much notice as possible.
Weekend and Holiday Overtime
Weekend or holiday overtime may be scheduled during peak automotive production cycles. Compensation for holiday work follows the guidelines in Section 5.4.

3.4 Remote Work (Staff-Only)
While the majority of KPM’s workforce is engaged in hands-on production roles that require on-site presence, certain administrative and support functions may be eligible for remote work arrangements.
Eligibility
Remote work may be available to employees in roles such as:
Human Resources
Accounting and Finance
Procurement
Scheduling and Planning
Engineering (non-production tasks)
Administrative support
Production-line employees, maintenance technicians, and logistics personnel are not eligible for remote work due to the nature of their responsibilities.
Approval Process
Remote work requires supervisor approval and may be granted on a temporary, part-time, or hybrid basis depending on business needs. HR may review remote work arrangements periodically to ensure compliance and effectiveness.
Expectations for Remote Employees
Employees approved for remote work must:
Maintain reliable internet access
Be available during core business hours
Follow all data-security and confidentiality policies (see Section 8)
Use company-approved tools and systems
Remain responsive to supervisors and team members
Ensure a safe and distraction-free work environment
Remote work is a privilege, not an entitlement, and may be modified or discontinued at any time based on performance, attendance, or operational requirements.

3.5 Shift Swaps and Schedule Changes
Employees may request a shift swap with another qualified employee in the same department. All shift swaps must be approved by a supervisor to ensure adequate coverage and compliance with overtime rules.
Supervisors may adjust schedules based on:
Production demands
Equipment availability
Customer orders
Staffing levels
Safety considerations
Employees will be notified of schedule changes as early as possible.

3.6 Inclement Weather and Emergency Closures
Kentucky weather can be unpredictable, and severe conditions may impact operations. KPM will communicate closures, delays, or modified schedules through:
Company email
Text alerts (if enrolled)
Supervisor notifications
Employees in essential roles may be required to report to work during inclement weather. Safety is always the top priority, and employees should use good judgment when traveling.

3.7 Time Away From Work
For information on paid time off, sick leave, FMLA, maternity/paternity leave, and other absences, employees should refer to Section 5: Benefits & Leave Policies.

4. Compensation & Payroll

4.1 Pay Schedule
Kentucky Precision Manufacturing (KPM) compensates employees on a bi-weekly payroll schedule, with paydays occurring every other Friday. This schedule supports predictable income planning for employees and aligns with standard practices in the automotive manufacturing industry.
Employees are encouraged to review their pay statements regularly to ensure accuracy. Any discrepancies should be reported to the Payroll Department within five business days of receiving the statement. KPM will investigate and correct errors promptly.
Direct Deposit
Direct deposit is the standard method of payment for all employees. Pay is electronically deposited into the employee’s designated bank account on payday. Employees may split deposits across multiple accounts if desired.
Employees who need to update their banking information must submit a new direct deposit authorization form to Payroll at least one pay cycle before the change takes effect.
Paper Checks (Exceptions Only)
Paper checks are issued only in limited circumstances, such as system outages or when direct deposit is not feasible. Employees receiving paper checks must pick them up in person and present valid identification.

4.2 Wage & Salary Administration
KPM is committed to maintaining a fair and competitive compensation structure that reflects the skills, responsibilities, and performance expectations of each role. Compensation decisions are based on:
Job classification and responsibilities
Market competitiveness within the automotive manufacturing sector
Employee experience and qualifications
Performance and contribution to team goals
Company financial performance
Annual Compensation Review
KPM conducts an annual compensation review to evaluate wage competitiveness and performance-based adjustments. Merit increases are not guaranteed and depend on:
Individual performance evaluations
Attendance and reliability
Safety compliance
Quality of work
Company profitability and budget
Supervisors will communicate any changes to compensation following the review cycle.
Shift Differentials
Employees assigned to evening, night, or weekend shifts may receive a shift differential. Differential rates vary by department and may be adjusted based on production needs or industry standards.

4.3 Timekeeping Requirements
Accurate timekeeping is essential for ensuring proper compensation and compliance with federal and state labor laws. KPM uses an electronic timekeeping system to record hours worked.
Clock-In and Clock-Out Procedures
Non-exempt employees must:
Clock in at the start of their shift
Clock out at the end of their shift
Clock in and out for meal breaks (if required by department)
Employees must not clock in earlier or clock out later than scheduled without supervisor approval. Time spent preparing for work (e.g., putting on PPE) should occur within scheduled hours unless otherwise directed.
Prohibited Timekeeping Practices
The following actions are strictly prohibited:
Falsifying time records
Clocking in or out for another employee (“buddy punching”)
Altering time entries without authorization
Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
Missed Punches
Employees who forget to clock in or out must notify their supervisor immediately. Supervisors will correct missed punches based on verified work hours.

4.4 Payroll Deductions
KPM is required to make certain deductions from employee paychecks. These deductions fall into two categories: mandatory and voluntary.
Mandatory Deductions
Mandatory deductions include:
Federal income tax
Kentucky state income tax
Social Security (FICA)
Medicare
Court-ordered garnishments (e.g., child support)
KPM complies with all legal requirements regarding garnishments and will notify employees when such deductions are applied.
Voluntary Deductions
Voluntary deductions may include:
Health, dental, and vision insurance premiums
401(k) retirement contributions
Supplemental insurance plans
Flexible spending accounts (if applicable)
Employees may adjust voluntary deductions during open enrollment or after qualifying life events (e.g., marriage, birth of a child).

4.5 Pay Adjustments and Corrections
KPM strives to ensure that all employees are paid accurately and on time. If an employee believes there is an error in their pay, they should notify Payroll immediately. Common issues may include:
Incorrect hours
Missing overtime
Incorrect deductions
Missing shift differentials
KPM will investigate and correct verified errors as quickly as possible, typically within the next pay cycle.
Overpayments
If an overpayment occurs, KPM will work with the employee to establish a reasonable repayment plan. Employees are expected to cooperate fully in resolving overpayments.

4.6 Wage Transparency and Confidentiality
Employees may discuss their wages with others if they choose, as protected by federal law. However, employees with access to payroll information as part of their job responsibilities (e.g., HR or Payroll staff) must maintain strict confidentiality and may not disclose wage information of others.

4.7 Travel Time and Reimbursement
Employees who travel for approved business purposes may be eligible for:
Mileage reimbursement
Meal allowances
Lodging reimbursement
Compensation for travel time (for non-exempt employees)
All travel must be pre-approved by a supervisor. Employees should refer to the company’s Travel and Expense Policy for detailed guidelines.

4.8 Bonuses and Incentive Pay
KPM may offer bonuses or incentive pay based on:
Production targets
Quality metrics
Safety performance
Attendance
Company profitability
Bonuses are not guaranteed and may vary from year to year based on business conditions and customer demand.

4.9 Pay During Plant Shutdowns or Reduced Operations
Automotive manufacturing may experience temporary shutdowns due to:
Customer production pauses
Supply chain disruptions
Equipment upgrades
Seasonal demand changes
During shutdowns, compensation will follow applicable laws and company policy. Employees may use available PTO during unpaid shutdown periods. Supervisors will communicate shutdown schedules as early as possible.

5. Benefits & Leave Policies
Kentucky Precision Manufacturing (KPM) offers a comprehensive benefits program designed to support the health, financial security, and overall well-being of our employees and their families. This section outlines the benefits available to eligible employees and explains the various types of leave provided under company policy and applicable laws.
Because benefits and leave policies often interact with one another (for example, maternity leave may overlap with FMLA), employees are encouraged to review related sections and consult HR for clarification when needed.

5.1 Health & Insurance Benefits
KPM provides a competitive benefits package to eligible full-time employees. Benefits are designed to promote physical health, financial stability, and peace of mind. Eligibility for most benefits begins on the first day of the month following 30 days of continuous full-time employment.
Medical Insurance
KPM offers a group medical insurance plan that includes coverage for:
Preventive care
Primary and specialist visits
Hospitalization
Emergency services
Prescription medications
Employees may choose from multiple plan options, including preferred provider organization (PPO) and high-deductible health plans (HDHP), depending on availability. Premiums are shared between the company and the employee.
Dental Insurance
Dental coverage includes preventive cleanings, basic restorative services, and major procedures. Orthodontic coverage may be available depending on the selected plan.
Vision Insurance
Vision benefits typically include annual eye exams, lenses, frames, and contact lenses. Discounts for laser vision correction may also be available.
Life and Accidental Death Insurance
KPM provides basic life insurance and accidental death and dismemberment (AD&D) coverage at no cost to eligible employees. Employees may purchase additional voluntary coverage for themselves or dependents.
Dependent Coverage
Employees may enroll eligible dependents—including spouses and children up to age 26—in medical, dental, and vision plans. Additional documentation may be required to verify dependent eligibility.
Benefits Summary Documents
Employees will receive detailed plan documents during onboarding and during annual open enrollment. These documents provide specific coverage details, exclusions, and claims procedures. Employees should refer to these documents for complete information.

5.2 Retirement Plans
KPM supports long-term financial planning through a 401(k) retirement savings plan. Eligible employees may contribute a percentage of their pay on a pre-tax or Roth basis, subject to IRS limits.
Employer Matching Contributions
KPM may offer a matching contribution based on company performance and budget. Matching contributions, if offered, will be communicated annually.
Vesting
Employee contributions are always 100% vested. Employer contributions may be subject to a vesting schedule as outlined in the plan documents.
Plan Administration
The retirement plan is administered by a third-party provider. Employees can manage contributions, investment options, and account information through the provider’s online portal.

5.3 Paid Time Off (PTO)
KPM provides Paid Time Off (PTO) to support work-life balance and allow employees to take time away from work for rest, personal needs, or illness.
Accrual
PTO accrues bi-weekly based on hours worked and years of service. Accrual rates are outlined in the PTO schedule provided during onboarding.
Using PTO
Employees should request PTO in advance through their supervisor. Approval is based on staffing needs, production schedules, and departmental workload. PTO may be used in hourly increments.
Carryover and Payout
PTO carryover limits and payout rules at separation are defined in the PTO policy. Employees should refer to the PTO guidelines or contact HR for details.
PTO During Peak Production
During high-demand periods—such as major automotive model launches—PTO availability may be limited. Supervisors will communicate blackout periods when necessary.

5.4 Holidays
KPM observes the following paid holidays:
New Year’s Day
Memorial Day
Independence Day
Labor Day
Thanksgiving Day
Day After Thanksgiving
Christmas Eve
Christmas Day
Holiday schedules may vary based on customer production calendars. Employees working on a company holiday may be eligible for holiday pay in accordance with company policy.

5.5 Sick Leave
Sick leave is provided to support employees who are unable to work due to illness, injury, or medical appointments.
Use of Sick Leave
Sick leave may be used for:
Personal illness or injury
Medical, dental, or vision appointments
Care for an immediate family member who is ill
Employees should notify their supervisor as soon as possible when using sick leave.
Documentation
A doctor’s note may be required for absences of three or more consecutive days, or when a pattern of absences suggests potential misuse.
Return-to-Work Clearance
Employees returning from extended illness or injury may be required to provide medical clearance, especially if the condition affects their ability to safely perform job duties.

5.6 Family & Medical Leave (FMLA)
Eligible employees may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) for qualifying reasons, including:
Birth or adoption of a child
Serious health condition of the employee
Care for a spouse, child, or parent with a serious health condition
Certain military-related needs
Eligibility Requirements
Employees must:
Have worked for KPM for at least 12 months
Have worked at least 1,250 hours in the previous 12 months
Work at a location with 50 or more employees within 75 miles
Coordination With Other Leave
FMLA may run concurrently with:
Maternity leave (Section 5.7)
Paternity leave (Section 5.8)
Workers’ compensation leave
PTO or sick leave
Employees should consult HR to understand how different leave types interact.
Benefits During FMLA
Employees may continue health insurance coverage during FMLA by paying their portion of premiums.

5.7 Maternity Leave
KPM supports employees during pregnancy, childbirth, and recovery through a combination of company leave and FMLA protections.
Leave Duration
Eligible employees may take maternity leave for:
Medical recovery (typically 6–8 weeks depending on delivery type)
Bonding time under FMLA (up to 12 weeks total leave)
Some portions of maternity leave may be paid depending on available PTO or short-term disability benefits.
Medical Documentation
Employees must provide medical certification indicating the expected due date and recovery period.
Job Protection
Employees returning from maternity leave will be reinstated to the same or an equivalent position, consistent with FMLA requirements.

5.8 Paternity Leave
KPM provides paternity leave to support employees welcoming a new child through birth or adoption.
Leave Duration
Eligible employees may take 1–2 weeks of paternity leave. This leave may be paid or unpaid depending on available PTO and company policy.
Coordination With FMLA
Paternity leave may run concurrently with FMLA if the employee is eligible.
Notification
Employees should notify their supervisor and HR as early as possible to plan for staffing needs.

5.9 Other Leave Types
Bereavement Leave
Employees may take paid leave following the death of an immediate family member. Additional unpaid leave may be granted at the supervisor’s discretion.
Jury Duty Leave
Employees summoned for jury duty will receive paid leave for scheduled work hours missed. Employees must provide a copy of the jury summons.
Military Leave (USERRA)
KPM complies with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA). Employees called to active duty or training will be granted leave and reinstated in accordance with federal law.
Voting Leave (Kentucky-Specific)
Kentucky law allows employees reasonable time off to vote. Employees must request voting leave in advance.

5.10 Leave of Absence (Non-Medical)
Employees may request an unpaid leave of absence for personal reasons not covered by other leave categories. Approval is at the discretion of HR and management and depends on business needs.

5.11 Interaction With Other Policies
Employees should refer to:
Section 3 for attendance expectations
Section 10 for disciplinary procedures related to attendance misuse
Section 7 for safety-related medical leave requirements
Benefits summary documents for insurance-specific rules
HR is available to help employees understand how different leave types interact.

6. Workplace Conduct & Expectations
Kentucky Precision Manufacturing (KPM) is committed to maintaining a workplace that is safe, respectful, productive, and aligned with the high standards expected in the automotive manufacturing industry. Every employee plays a role in upholding these expectations. This section outlines the behaviors, responsibilities, and standards required to ensure a positive and professional work environment.

6.1 Code of Conduct
KPM expects all employees to conduct themselves in a manner that reflects professionalism, integrity, and respect. Because we supply precision components to major automotive manufacturers, our reputation depends not only on the quality of our products but also on the conduct of our workforce.
Professional Behavior
Employees are expected to:
Treat coworkers, supervisors, customers, and visitors with courtesy and respect
Communicate clearly and professionally
Follow instructions and established procedures
Maintain a positive and cooperative attitude
Avoid disruptive or inappropriate behavior
Professionalism applies equally to production employees, staff roles, and supervisors.
Integrity and Ethical Conduct
Employees must act honestly and ethically in all business dealings. This includes:
Accurately reporting time worked
Protecting company property and confidential information
Avoiding conflicts of interest
Reporting unethical behavior
Employees should refer to Section 8 for technology and data-related conduct expectations.
Conflicts of Interest
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests. Examples include:
Accepting gifts from suppliers
Using company resources for personal gain
Engaging in outside employment that interferes with KPM responsibilities
Employees should disclose potential conflicts to HR.

6.2 Anti-Harassment & Anti-Bullying Policy
KPM is committed to providing a workplace free from harassment, bullying, and discrimination. These behaviors undermine morale, productivity, and safety — all critical in a high-precision manufacturing environment.
Prohibited Conduct
Harassment may include:
Unwelcome comments or jokes
Offensive gestures or images
Sexual advances or requests for favors
Derogatory remarks about protected characteristics
Intimidation or threats
Bullying may include:
Verbal abuse
Deliberate exclusion
Sabotaging another employee’s work
Spreading rumors
Excessive criticism or unreasonable demands
Reporting Harassment or Bullying
Employees who experience or witness harassment or bullying should report it immediately to:
Their supervisor
HR
Any member of management
Reports may be made verbally or in writing. Anonymous reports may also be submitted through the company’s reporting channel (details provided during onboarding).
Investigation Process
KPM will:
Investigate all complaints promptly and confidentially
Interview relevant parties
Review evidence
Take appropriate corrective action
Non-Retaliation
Retaliation against employees who report concerns or participate in investigations is strictly prohibited. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

6.3 Drug & Alcohol Policy
Because KPM operates in a safety-sensitive automotive manufacturing environment, maintaining a drug- and alcohol-free workplace is essential.
Prohibited Behavior
Employees may not:
Use, possess, or distribute illegal drugs on company property
Report to work under the influence of drugs or alcohol
Consume alcohol during work hours or breaks
Misuse prescription medications
Drug and Alcohol Testing
Testing may be required:
Pre-employment
Post-accident
When reasonable suspicion exists
Randomly (for safety-sensitive positions)
Employees who refuse testing may be subject to disciplinary action.
Prescription Medications
Employees taking prescribed medications that may impair performance must notify HR or their supervisor. KPM will work with the employee to determine appropriate accommodations.

6.4 Workplace Violence Prevention
KPM has zero tolerance for workplace violence. A safe environment is essential for maintaining productivity and meeting customer expectations.
Prohibited Conduct
Workplace violence includes:
Threats or intimidation
Physical altercations
Harassment or stalking
Possession of weapons on company property (unless legally permitted and approved)
Aggressive or hostile behavior
Reporting Concerns
Employees must report threats or violent behavior immediately to:
A supervisor
HR
Security personnel (if applicable)
KPM will investigate all reports and take appropriate action.

6.5 Dress Code & Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
KPM maintains a dress code to ensure safety, professionalism, and compliance with customer and regulatory requirements.
Production Employees
Production employees must wear:
Company-issued uniforms (if applicable)
Steel-toe or composite-toe safety shoes
Safety glasses
Gloves, hearing protection, or other PPE as required
Hairnets or beard nets in designated areas
Loose clothing, jewelry, or accessories that could become entangled in machinery are prohibited.
Staff and Administrative Employees
Employees in office or administrative roles should dress in business-casual attire. Clothing should be neat, clean, and appropriate for a professional environment.
Special Requirements
Certain areas of the facility may require additional PPE based on:
OSHA regulations
Customer audits
Internal safety assessments
Employees must follow all posted PPE requirements and instructions from supervisors.

6.6 Use of Company Property
Employees are responsible for using company property appropriately and safeguarding it from damage or theft.
Examples of Company Property
Tools and equipment
Computers and mobile devices
Vehicles
Office supplies
Manufacturing materials
Prohibited Uses
Employees may not:
Use company property for personal projects
Remove property from the premises without authorization
Tamper with or disable safety devices
Damage or misuse equipment
Misuse of company property may result in disciplinary action.

6.7 Confidentiality and Protection of Company Information
KPM employees may have access to sensitive information, including:
Production processes
Customer specifications
Pricing and contracts
Employee records
Proprietary technology
Employees must protect confidential information and may not disclose it to unauthorized individuals. This requirement continues even after employment ends.
For technology-specific confidentiality rules, see Section 8: Technology & Data Policies.

6.8 Social Media and Public Representation
Employees must use good judgment when discussing work-related topics on social media.
Guidelines

10. Disciplinary Action & Termination
Kentucky Precision Manufacturing (KPM) is committed to maintaining a safe, respectful, and productive workplace. To support this commitment, KPM uses a fair and consistent disciplinary process that addresses performance issues, policy violations, and unsafe behavior.
The goal of disciplinary action is not punishment — it is to correct behavior, reinforce expectations, and support employees in meeting performance and conduct standards. However, when violations are serious or repeated, disciplinary action may result in termination of employment.

10.2 Progressive Discipline Process
KPM generally follows a progressive discipline model. However, the company reserves the right to skip steps or move directly to termination depending on the severity of the violation.
The typical steps in the progressive discipline process are:

1. Verbal Warning
A supervisor discusses the issue with the employee, explains expectations, and documents the conversation. The goal is early correction and clarity.

2. Written Warning
If the issue continues or is more serious, the supervisor issues a written warning outlining:
The specific concern
Expected improvements
A timeline for improvement
Consequences of continued issues
The employee may be asked to sign the warning to acknowledge receipt.

3. Final Written Warning or Suspension
For repeated violations or more serious concerns, KPM may issue a final written warning or place the employee on unpaid suspension. This step signals that immediate improvement is required.

4. Termination of Employment
If the employee fails to meet expectations after prior warnings — or commits a serious violation — employment may be terminated.
Documentation
All disciplinary actions are documented and placed in the employee’s personnel file, consistent with Section 2.4.
